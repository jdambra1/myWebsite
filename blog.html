Describing methods, skills and resources needed to complete digital projects successfully – what do you need to make projects successful? What tools? What skills?

There are various skills you can employ to ensure the completion of a digital project. If the project is a group one then communication will be a key skill. Time keeping is an essential skill to keep to deadlines. This will affect many parts of a project such as completion time. The delays will potentially cost money and risk the reputation of the business.


Methods


Waterfall is a common method used by modern businesses. The definition is as follows “Waterfall is a linear process for design and development, where line items for creating the software are set and completed in order. Put succinctly, once one step is complete, the team moves onto the next step. The team typically can’t go back a step without starting over from the beginning of the process, as each step is contingent on the ones leading up to it.” [1] This is similar to critical path management and ensures streamlining in a business. Once tasks are determined and whichever task is dependant on anothers completion are ordered logically to ensure the most effective route to completion. For example if you were to make a cup of tea you have to add the boiling water to the tea bag in the mug before you add milk. Waterfall adding the milk before anything else is done in the task of making a cup of tea.


Kanban is another method employed by businesses to ensure the completion of digital projects. The definition is as follows “Kanban is a visual system for managing work as it moves through a process. Kanban visualises both the process (the workflow) and the actual work passing through that process. The goal of Kanban is to identify potential bottlenecks in your process and fix them so work can flow through it cost-effectively at an optimal speed or throughput.” [2] As stated above Kanban is a visual process. This gives the user an opportunity to have a full visual of the project broken down allowing them to see any possibilities of streamlining or potential problems down the line.

A list of resources a business could use includes “Digital projects come in all shapes and sizes; Website, Mobile app, Video, Game, Event, Content, E-commerce, Social media, Advertising, Search engine optimisation” [3] As you can see there are plenty of resources a business can use for digital projects. Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects – How does GDPR impact digital projects? 


GDPR is the General Data Protection Regulation. “What GDPR means is that citizens of the EU and EEA now have greater control over their personal data and assurances that their information is being securely protected across Europe.” [4]


This means that any digital project that involves handling a consumers personal data must take GDPR into consideration. superoffice.com suggest these 5 steps to ensure compliance in any digital project “Map your project’s data, Determine what data you need to keep, Put security measures in place, Review your documentation and Establish procedures for handling personal data “ [5] If these 5 steps are applied to an existing or new project then they will be GDPR compliant. Please visit the website for more detail on each step.


Describing copyright and other constraints affecting websites


First a definition of copyright is “Copyright refers to the legal protection of original works of authorship. This includes literary, written, dramatic, artistic, musical, and other types of creative expression.” [6] Therefore you must be careful to publish any material that may be copyrighted.


You will need to ensure that any written publications you are posting are copyright free or that you have explicit permission from the author to share it. The same is for images. Any images you may use on websites must be either original ie copyright owned (any photo you take is instantly subject to copy-write or you could employ a photographer/ graphic designer etc) or you could use a stock photo website and pay. There are some free stock photo websites like pixabay.com that have a massive library of royalty free images you could use.

Describe which are the appropriate filetypes for websites


The appropriate filetypes for websites are “.AVI | .CSS | .DOC | .EXE | .GIF | .HTML/ .HTM | .JPG/ .JPEG | .MID/ .MIDI | .MP3 | .MPG/ .MPEG | .MOV/ .QT | .PDF | .PNG | .RAM | .RAR | .TIFF | .TXT | .WAV | .ZIP | Streaming Media ]” [7]


These file types are necessary to use in a website or it will not work. You can convert other kinds of files to the aforementioned types to make them compatible with your website. For example if you have a PSD (photoshop document) and do not convert that into a JPEG or a PNG it will not work on the website as it is an inappropriate file type.
